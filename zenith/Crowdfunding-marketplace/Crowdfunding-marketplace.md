### Crowdfunding Business & Its Types

Crowdfunding is raising funds from the masses for a particular venture like a campaign, charity, financial help, startup, etc.

It helps small businesses or startups to raise money in exchange for rewards, debt, equity, or nothing at all.

Here are the most common types of business crowdfunding:

*   **Donation crowdfunding:** This type of crowdfunding is run by non-profit organizations. Taking up money from the masses as a form of a donation to help venture & give nothing in return.
*   **Equity crowdfunding:** This is the most commonly used crowdfunding type. You sell a part of your business to an investor(s) and they provide you with the funds to move ahead.
*   **Debt crowdfunding:** Under this one, business owners borrow money from other individuals, instead of from a bank. The amount is borrowed on a set annual percentage rate as that of a bank's loan.
*   **Rewards crowdfunding:** This type is a recent evolution in the market. The fundraisers offer rewards to the donators in exchange for the donation made. The reward could be in the form of merchandise, gift, product, or service.

### Crowdfunding Marketplace

The rise of platforms like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and GoFundMe has brought crowdfunding into the mainstream business model.

People have started taking it seriously & are running a successful business around it.

A crowdfunding marketplace is a platform where multiple prospects can start their crowdfunding project.

Every member can choose a funding goal and set the number of days to reach that goal. They can set their own storyline & challenges to share with the audience.
